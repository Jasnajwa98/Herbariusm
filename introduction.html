History

The establishment of Herbarium in the School of Biological Sciences, USM, is in accordance with the practical implementation for the Botany courses. Since 1971, voucher specimen collection has been donations from fellow lecturers as record-keeping for research and lesson references.


Herbarium, USM was originally located in Room 03-05, Ground Floor, Building G08, School of Biological Sciences. However, Herbarium has relocated and can be found in Room 105-109, 1st Floor, Building D32, Industrial Biotechnology Research Laboratory, Universiti Sains Malaysia since 2009.

Function

The function of Herbarium in USM is to prepare voucher specimens to act as dried specimen collections for species taxonomy references, data provisions for lessons and research, manages practical classes, and in conducting of Herbarium Techniques workshops.


Furthermore, Herbarium is essential in supporting all programmes and efforts in research and services, where one of its founding objectives is to be the main referral centre in Northern Peninsular Malaysia.


Up until now, the herbarium’s collection is over 11623 species covering up to 200 families. Among the available collections are bryophytes, ferns, gymnosperms, and angiosperms. These voucher specimens are arranged according to its families, and these collections involve research specimens by fellow lecturers, postgraduate students, and collections that are used as teaching materials. Immense research activities increase the collection over time.


The database and record management system used in Herbarium is Warehouse Management System, which is an affordable and simple system that gives a clear view on ‘warehouse’ storage in terms of planning, execution, and the effectiveness of the whole herbarium operations.
